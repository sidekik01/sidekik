# Sidekik UX Blueprint

## Screen 1 — First Run Experience

### 1. Welcome Screen

Purpose: Introduce Sidekik with one clear next step.

Elements:
- Logo
- Tagline: The creative sidekick behind every great video.
- Primary CTA: Build My Brand
- Secondary CTA: Skip for Now

UX Notes:
- Keep the screen calm and spacious.
- Make the primary CTA visually dominant.
- Keep the secondary CTA quiet and low-pressure.

### 2. Creator Type Screen

Question:

How do you create?

Options:
- Creator
- Agency
- Brand
- Social Team

UX Notes:
- Present options as simple selectable cards.
- Avoid long descriptions unless needed later.
- Make selection feel lightweight and reversible.

### 3. Brand Builder Screen

Question:

Let's build your first brand.

Steps:
- Upload logo
- Choose primary color
- Choose highlight color
- Choose caption preset
- Choose default platform
- Review brand

UX Notes:
- Use a step-by-step flow with one decision per screen or panel.
- Show a compact live preview as brand choices are made.
- Let users skip optional brand details without blocking progress.

### 4. First Project Screen

Question:

Ready to finish your first video?

Action:

Upload your first video.

UX Notes:
- Make upload the obvious next action.
- Support drag and drop and click-to-upload.
- Keep supporting copy minimal.

### 5. Analysis Transition

Copy:

I'll take it from here.

States:
- Reading metadata
- Finding the hook
- Checking pacing
- Preparing captions

UX Notes:
- Use calm progress motion.
- Communicate that Sidekik is actively preparing the project.
- Avoid technical jargon during the transition.

### 6. Design Principles

- Calm
- Premium
- Minimal
- One obvious next step
- No clutter

# Screen 2 — Editor Experience

## Philosophy

The editor should feel like a premium creative workspace.

The video is always the primary focus.

Every control should exist to help the user finish their video faster.

---

## Layout

- Top Header
- Left Sidebar
- Center Workspace
- Right Sidebar
- Bottom Timeline

---

## Header

Left:
- sidekik logo

Center:
- Current Project Name

Right:
- Creative Score
- Save Project
- Export

---

## Left Sidebar

- Workspace
- Brands
- Projects
- Assets
- Templates
- Settings

The sidebar should collapse.

---

## Center Workspace

The uploaded video should occupy approximately 70% of the available width.

Below the video:

- Transport Controls
- Timeline
- Zoom
- Current Time

The video always remains visible while editing.

---

## Right Sidebar

Modes:

- Analyze
- Transcript
- Style
- Sidekik
- Export

Only one mode can be open at a time.

Each mode answers one question.

---

## Timeline

Timeline should become the primary editing surface.

Eventually it will include:

- Caption Blocks
- Waveform
- Markers
- Playhead
- Zoom

Future:
- B-roll markers

---

## Empty States

No Project:

Upload your first video.

No Transcript:

Generate Transcript.

No Captions:

Generate Captions.

No Style:

Choose a Caption Preset.

---

## Design Rules

The video is always the hero.

Never overwhelm.

Every interaction should require as few clicks as possible.

Advanced controls stay hidden until needed.

Everything should feel calm, fast, and intentional.

# Screen 3 — Sidekik

## Purpose

This is the heart of the application.

Instead of presenting settings, Sidekik reviews the project and guides the creator toward a better result.

The experience should feel like sitting next to an experienced creative director.

Never overwhelming.

Never robotic.

Always helpful.

---

## Layout

Top:

- Creative Score
- Project Summary

Middle:

- Strengths
- Opportunities
- Warnings

Bottom:

- Quick Actions
- Ask Sidekik

---

## Greeting

When analysis finishes:

"I watched your video."

Never say:

"Analysis Complete"

Never say:

"AI Finished"

Always sound human.

---

## Creative Score

Display:

- Overall Score
- Hook
- Readability
- Pacing
- Captions
- CTA
- Accessibility
- Platform Optimization

---

## Strengths

Examples:

- Strong opening hook.
- Good pacing.
- Easy-to-read captions.
- Good speaking speed.
- Captions remain inside safe zones.

---

## Opportunities

Examples:

- Caption 7 is slightly long.
- CTA appears too late.
- Speaking pace increases near the end.
- Highlight stronger action words.

---

## Warnings

Examples:

- First 2 seconds contain no speech.
- Caption density may be high for mobile.
- No ending CTA detected.

---

## Quick Actions

- Fix Reading Speed
- Shorten Long Captions
- Improve Caption Timing
- Highlight Suggested Words
- Optimize For TikTok
- Optimize For LinkedIn
- Optimize For Meta Ads

Each Quick Action should eventually become a one-click AI workflow.

---

## Ask Sidekik

This is not a generic chatbot.

It already knows:

- Project
- Transcript
- Caption Blocks
- Brand
- Creative Score
- Platform
- Style

Users can ask:

- How can I improve this?
- Would this work better for Instagram?
- Make my captions more punchy.
- Rewrite this for LinkedIn.
- Improve my hook.
- Suggest a stronger CTA.

---

## Personality

Never over-excited.

Never says:

"Amazing!"

"Awesome!"

"Incredible!"

Instead:

"I found three opportunities to improve retention."

"This hook could become stronger."

"I'd recommend shortening these captions."

Always calm.

Always professional.

Always confident.

---

## Product Principle

The Sidekik screen should become the reason creators subscribe.
